Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 2026

This page explains what happens with your data when you play GrimWar. The short version: we collect very little, we don't store anything permanently, and we don't sell your information.

What We Collect

Display names — When you join a game, you pick a name. That name is shared with other players in your session so they know who they're playing against. It's not saved anywhere after the game ends.

IP addresses — GrimWar uses peer-to-peer connections (WebRTC) to connect players directly. This means your IP address may be visible to other players in your game session. We don't operate any servers that log or store IP addresses.

Game data — Things like your score, spell choices, and cosmetic settings only exist while you're playing. Nothing is saved between sessions.

Advertising

We show ads through Google AdSense to keep the game free. Google uses cookies to figure out which ads to show you, based on sites you've visited before. If you'd rather not see personalized ads, you can change that in Google's ad settings or opt out at aboutads.info.

Cookies

The game itself doesn't set any cookies. Google AdSense does use cookies for ad serving. That's it.

Updates

If we change this policy, we'll update this page. Nothing fancy.

Questions?

If something here doesn't make sense or you want to know more, reach out at contact@grimwar.net.
